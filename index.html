About DevilSec



DevilSec was founded as a student group at ASU to socialize, network, and learn the current industry standard tools of the cybersecurity field. The group serves as an entry point for students of computer science and other majors to compensate for the lack of current offensive cybersecurity curriculum universities' lack or otherwise teach using dated and irrelevant information and resources.

DevilSec holds weekly formal meetings throughout the Fall and Spring semesters that cover a range of topics starting from using a virtual machine with Linux, to learning different tools such as web proxies and port scanners, and eventually to utilizing actual exploits for vulnerabilities in practice labs to get hands on experience. The group also has informal meetings for members that want to walk though a machine on HackTheBox or just be around to chat and answer questions.

What we Offer?


Presentations

Weekly educational presentations on a variety of pertinent offensive and defensive security topics, usually paired with a custom lab.

Guest Talks

Guest talks by a variety of infosec professionals providing industry insight and detailed presentations on a multitude of subjects.

Networking

Network with recruiters, industry professionals and fellow cybersecurity students within our Discord community.

Competitions

Compete in renowned collegiate competitions such as CCDC where you can put your skills to the test in a real world environment and network with sponsors.
